Three more specifics

Sourced 250,000+ offline point-of-sale records from Nielsen and NPD. Data that had never been inside Amazon's systems. Transformed them into structured training datasets for ML models. That pipeline surfaced products that internal data alone would never have found. $450M in annualized GMV.

Went outside the building for data when internal sources weren't enough

Two teams at Amazon were building ML models for the same catalog, independently. One for product recommendations, one for brand tagging. Different schemas, different pipelines, duplicated infrastructure. Wrote the shared architecture spec that unified both. The hard part wasn't technical. It was getting two teams who were used to working alone to agree on shared definitions. Once they did, models could be reused across both and new features shipped in half the time.

The hard part was alignment, not architecture

Amazon's product catalog had the same brand listed hundreds of different ways across 400 million records. Samsung, SAMSUNG, Samsung Electronics, samsung corp. Built the pipeline that consolidated over a million brand identities into clean, deduplicated records. Every ML model that depended on brand data got more accurate without being retouched. 12% fewer labeling defects across the entire catalog.

Fixed the data once. Every system downstream got better automatically.
